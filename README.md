***Language Data & Structural Systems Portfolio***

Structural Linguistics, System Architecture & Applied Computational Modeling

This repository presents selected case studies and technical projects demonstrating how structural linguistic reasoning can inform system design, classification logic, and scalable decision architectures.

Rather than approaching linguistics as interpretive analysis alone, this portfolio frames it as a modeling discipline capable of shaping structured, governable, and scalable systems. The materials combine conceptual modeling, operational restructuring, and computational implementation.

***Repository Structure***

**Case 1 — Speech Analytics & Structural Categorization: From Raw Interaction to Structured Insight**

Problem: Customer interaction data lacked coherent semantic architecture. Categories were reactive and inconsistently applied.

Intervention:
• Linguistic feature identification
• Taxonomy restructuring
• Hierarchical categorization logic
• Annotation consistency design

Contribution: Demonstrates how linguistic structuring stabilizes analytical systems and prevents category drift.

<!-->

**Case 2 — Ethical Hallucinations & Structural Risk: Error as Architecture, Not Accident**

Problem: Model hallucinations were treated as isolated anomalies rather than systemic failures.

Reframing: Hallucination analyzed as:
• Classification breakdown
• Constraint misalignment
• Epistemic instability

Contribution: Introduces structural error typology and governance-aware evaluation of automated outputs.

<!-->

**Case 3 — From Syntactic Trees to Process Trees: Hierarchical Modeling Under Scale**

Problem: Operational workflows expanded horizontally, leading to duplication and fragile updates.

Structural Transfer: Inspired by syntactic dependency trees:
• Hierarchical restructuring of decision flows
• Multi-branch (non-binary) decision nodes
• Context-sensitive subtrees (user-type dependent logic)
• Controlled growth preventing combinatorial collapse

Result: Operational complexity reorganized into scalable, traceable architecture. This case demonstrates abstraction transfer from formal linguistics to large-scale service systems.
