1. EPISTEMOLOGICAL POSITIONING: FROM VARIATION TO GOVERNANCE

Research Proposition: This applied research investigates how natural language variation in customer-service interactions creates semantic gaps in information retrieval systems. By analyzing speech analytics data and search logs, the project develops a methodological framework for transforming spontaneous speech patterns into governed corporate taxonomy, demonstrating how linguistic analysis can bridge human communication with computational systems.

In large-scale telecommunications customer service, the disconnect between how customers express problems and how systems understand those expressions creates operational inefficiencies and user frustration. This research addresses a fundamental challenge in applied computational linguistics: how to systematically analyze and structure natural language variation to improve human-machine communication. The work develops from a localized controlled vocabulary to an enterprise-wide taxonomy, providing a replicable model for linguistic infrastructure development in corporate environments.

2. RESEARCH CONTEXT: REAL DATA, REAL CONSEQUENCES

The Empirical Challenge

  • Data Source: Speech analytics transcripts + CRM search logs from multinational telecom (2020-2025)
  • Scale: 30,000+ customer service agents, 2M+ monthly interactions
  • Problem: Semantic noise between customer expressions, agent searches, and system content
  • Consequence: Operational inefficiency, customer frustration, and systemic communication failure
  • Linguistic Variance: Proliferation of non-standard technical jargon and regional idiomatic expressions across decentralized operational hubs.

Methodological Approach

  • Speech Analytics Analysis: Systematic examination of customer-agent communication patterns
  • Semantic Gap Mapping: Identifying divergences between expressed needs and system responses
  • Controlled Vocabulary Development: Creating structured terminological systems with semantic relations
  • Taxonomic Expansion: Scaling localized vocabulary to enterprise-wide knowledge infrastructure
  • Cross-Disciplinary Synthesis: Integrating linguistics, information science, and organizational design

METHODOLOGICAL ANALYSIS & RESEARCH INSIGHTS

  • Corpus Linguistics Methodology: This raw data extraction phase was not about quantifying error but about treating operational language as a corpus for systematic linguistic analysis.
  • Attested Usage as Evidence: The 41 variants were not "mistakes" but attested usage data revealing the semantic field's boundaries and points of friction between natural language and system requirements.
  • Semantic Field Mapping: Systematic documentation of lexical variants enables reconstruction of the conceptual boundaries of operational terminology.
  • Friction Point Identification: The gap between customer expressions, agent searches, and system classifications reveals specific placement for terminological intervention.
CRITICAL FINDING: THE AGENT'S DILEMMA

Agents developed their own intermediary vocabulary (12 search patterns) that neither matched customer expressions (41 variants) nor aligned perfectly with system classifications (3 terms). This created a "triple mismatch" requiring agents to constantly translate between customer language, their own search language, and system terminology‚Äîadding cognitive load and reducing efficiency.

Context: Confronted with attested variation, the task shifted from description to prescriptive normalization.


This phase involved analyzing the variant corpus to distinguish between synonymy (different terms for the same concept, e.g., "franquia" and "dados m√≥veis") and contextual or inaccurate usage (e.g., "3G" as a metonym for mobile data).

The goal was to establish a preferred term around which a controlled vocabulary could be structured.

METHODOLOGICAL ANALYSIS & RESEARCH INSIGHTS

  • Corpus-Based Terminology Work: This stage operationalizes corpus-based terminology work, in which decisions are not imposed but arrived naturally from usage patterns and operational needs.
  • Usage-Driven Decision Making: Term selection emerges from systematic analysis of frequency, distribution patterns, and operational relevance rather than prescriptive imposition.
  • Systemic Interoperability: The requirement for terms to function across search systems, knowledge bases, and agent training materials drives selection criteria.
  • Governance Artifact Creation: The "terms to avoid" list serves as both a governance tool and a boundary object between linguistic analysis and system design, making terminological decisions explicit and implementable.
Context: The final stage materializes the analysis into reusable knowledge infrastructure. The selected preferred term, "franquia" ("data allowance"), required formal definition and explicit placement within the growing corporate taxonomy.

This transforms an unstable, folk-concept into a governed taxonomic node with clear intension (definition) and extension (position in the hierarchy).

METHODOLOGICAL ANALYSIS & RESEARCH INSIGHTS

  • Dual Output Framework: This phase demonstrates the dual output of applied terminological work: a conceptual specification (the definition) and a structural specification (the taxonomic position).
  • Conceptual Specification: The formal definition establishes the term's intension‚Äîits precise conceptual boundaries and denotational criteria.
  • Structural Specification: The taxonomic position establishes the term's extension‚Äîits place within the broader knowledge hierarchy and its relations to other concepts.
  • Scalable Knowledge Infrastructure: Together, these specifications ensure the term is both meaningful in isolation and functional within a system, enabling precise retrieval, consistent training, and scalable knowledge management.
  • Applied Linguistics as Engineering: The case of "franquia" exemplifies how linguistic analysis directly engineers informational clarity, transforming unstable folk-concepts into governed technical artifacts.

5. ETHICAL METHODOLOGY: GOVERNANCE AS RESEARCH PRACTICE

Methodological Innovation: Ethical considerations were embedded as decision criteria at each pipeline stage, transforming ethics from external commentary to internal governance mechanism. This approach recognizes that in linguistic data science, ethical decisions are technical decisions with structural consequences.

Inclusion as Data Criterion

All 41 legitimate linguistic variants were included as valid data points, rejecting the notion of "correct" vs. "incorrect" formulations in natural language use.


Technical Implementation:
Synonym rings with usage frequency weighting

Transparency in Hierarchy

Taxonomic structures were designed with explicit rationale documentation, making hierarchical decisions and their implications visible and contestable.

Technical Implementation:
Governance metadata attached to all hierarchical relationships

Democratic Governance Structures

A Corporate Language Committee with cross-functional representation was established to make ongoing terminology decisions
