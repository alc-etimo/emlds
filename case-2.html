1. EPISTEMOLOGICAL POSITIONING: FROM HALLUCINATION TO INVESTIGATION

Research Proposition: This study investigates recurrent patterns in AI-generated subtitles for streaming platforms. It treats output anomalies not as merely technical errors but as traces of the underlying training data composition. The research develops methodological approaches for tracing data provenance through linguistic and cultural artifacts found in the commercial use AI in such contexts.

In industrial-scale automated localization, some generated content patterns dismissed as "hallucinations" reveal traceable connections to training data sources and curation practices. This research addresses a core methodological question in linguistic data science: how can we systematically trace the provenance of training data through the analysis of model outputs? The focus is on developing and maturing replicable techniques for identifying and classifying data source artifacts.

Complex Provenance in Volunteer Localization Networks

The Amara.org case exists within a broader, more complex ecosystem of volunteer-created localization data, most notably represented by fansubs (fan-subtitled content). This phenomenon introduces additional layers of methodological complexity for provenance analysis:

The Fansubs Provenance Chain
  • Source Material: Often begins with content distributed through informal channels
  • Labor: Translated by volunteers ("fansubbers") motivated by community participation
  • Distribution: Shared within fan communities via dedicated platforms
  • Data Harvesting: Potentially incorporated into web-crawled training datasets
  • Commercial Output: May appear in paid streaming platform subtitles
Methodological Challenges
  • Cascading Provenance: Multiple layers of unattributed source material
  • Labor Obfuscation: Volunteer work detached from original context
  • Licensing Ambiguity: Complex copyright status of source and derivative works
  • Attribution Loss: Systematic removal of fan credits in dataset processing
  • Semantic Drift & Bias: Loss of context and pragmatic intent leads to hallucinations in models.

Research Implications: The fansubs ecosystem exemplifies the challenge of cascading data provenance in AI training. It demonstrates how content can pass through multiple informal distribution and labor layers before entering commercial training pipelines, creating particularly complex chains for forensic reconstruction. This case highlights the need for more sophisticated provenance-tracing methodologies capable of detecting and analyzing multi-layered data sources in AI outputs.

For Linguistic Data Science, this represents a compelling research direction: developing computational methods to detect the linguistic and paratextual signatures of cascading provenance in large-scale datasets, contributing to both transparency in AI development and understanding of digital labor networks.

5. METHODOLOGICAL FRAMEWORK: ANALYSIS AS RESEARCH PRACTICE

Methodological Innovation: Analytical criteria were embedded at each investigation stage, transforming output analysis from observational reporting to systematic research practice. This approach recognizes that in training data provenance research, methodological decisions determine evidential validity and research reproducibility.

Inclusion as Analytical Criterion

All legitimate linguistic variants and cultural artifacts were treated as valid data points, rejecting simplistic notions of "correct" vs. "incorrect" in the analysis.

Technical Implementation: Comprehensive source documentation and pattern recognition protocols

Transparency in Classification

Artifact classifications included explicit rationale documentation, making analytical decisions and their evidential basis visible and methodologically contestable.

Technical Implementation: Governance metadata attached to all classification decisions

Contextual Analytical Framework

Each artifact was analyzed within its specific cultural, economic, and platform context, avoiding universalist assumptions about pattern significance.

Technical Implementation: Contextual annotation layers in analysis database

Reproducibility Protocol Design

Methodologies were designed for replication, with clear documentation of analytical steps, decision criteria, and evidence evaluation processes.

Technical Implementation: Step-by-step analytical protocols with evidence chains

7. ACADEMIC POSITIONING: FORWARD-LOOKING RESEARCH AGENDA

For EMLDS Committee: This case study documents occurrences of linguistic hallucination within Automated Speech Recognition (ASR) systems, ranging from repetitive verbatim loops to errors in major streaming platforms' interfaces. The analysis focuses on stochastic misalignment, where models generate textual sequences that lack phonetic or semantic correspondence with the original audio signal. These examples illustrate the boundaries of algorithmic reliability and underscore the critical necessity for human oversight in linguistic data processing workflows.
